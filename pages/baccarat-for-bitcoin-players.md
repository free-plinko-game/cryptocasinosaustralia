### Basics of Baccarat

Baccarat is a comparing card game played between two hands: the "player" and the "banker." Each baccarat coup (round of play) has three possible outcomes: "player" (the player has the higher score), "banker" (the banker has the higher score), and "tie." The game is played with one or more standard 52-card decks, and it is primarily a game of chance.

- **Objective**: The primary objective in baccarat is to bet on which hand, the player or the banker, will have a total value closest to nine.
- **Card Values**: Cards two through nine are worth their face value, tens and face cards are worth zero, and aces are worth one. If a hand’s total exceeds nine, only the second digit counts (e.g., a hand totaling 15 is valued at 5).
- **Gameplay**: Both the player and the banker are initially dealt two cards, and in some situations, a third card may be drawn based on set rules.

### Playing Strategies

Though baccarat relies heavily on chance, players often employ strategies to potentially increase their odds of success. Common strategies include:

- **Betting on the Banker**: Since the banker bet traditionally has a slightly lower house edge, players often favor this option. However, a commission is usually applied to banker wins.
- **Avoiding the Tie Bet**: The tie bet carries a significantly higher house edge, making it a less favorable option for many players.
