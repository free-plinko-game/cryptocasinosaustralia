### Origins and Television Fame

Plinko first appeared on "The Price Is Right" in January 1983. Designed by Frank Wayne, the game quickly became one of the show's most beloved segments. The game involves contestants dropping a small disc, known as a "chip," from the top of a vertical board. The board is filled with a grid of pegs, causing the chip to bounce unpredictably before landing in one of several slots at the bottom, each with varying cash amounts.

The allure of Plinko lies in its combination of skill and chance. While contestants choose where to drop the chip, the final outcome is largely determined by the peg layout and gravity. This balance allowed Plinko to captivate audiences, contributing to its long-standing popularity.

### Transition to Digital Platforms

As internet technology advanced, many traditional games found new life on digital platforms, and Plinko was no exception. The game's simple mechanics translated easily to an online format, where users could experience the thrill of dropping chips virtually. Early digital versions sought to replicate the television experience, complete with animated boards and varying prize amounts.
