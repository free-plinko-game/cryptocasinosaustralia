### Gameplay Mechanics

The game board in Mines typically consists of a grid where players must uncover tiles to progress, avoiding hidden mines. The exact size of the grid can vary, but it often ranges from a 3x3 to a larger 5x5 grid. The key objective is to clear as many safe tiles as possible without selecting a tile containing a mine.

- **Tile Selection**: Players choose tiles to click on, each revealing either a safe spot or a mine. If a mine is uncovered, the player loses the game immediately.
  
- **Remaining Mines Count**: Unlike traditional Minesweeper, each row in the game's interface sometimes displays the number of remaining mines, providing a strategic element as players decide which tiles might be safer to reveal.

- **Winning and Cash Out**: For each safe tile unveiled, players can opt to either continue uncovering tiles or cash out the accrued winnings. The potential payout increases as more tiles are revealed without hitting a mine, but so does the risk.

- **Strategy Application**: While Mines incorporates chance, strategic play can improve the odds. Players might begin by revealing tiles on the grid edges, as these typically have fewer adjacent mines, gradually working toward the center as patterns emerge.
